The Mineral Industry of Congo (Kinshasa)
By Thomas R. Yager

Note: In this chapter, information for 2020 is followed by information for 2021.


The Democratic Republic of the Congo [Congo (Kinshasa)]
played a globally significant role in the world's production of cobalt, copper, diamond, gold, tantalum, and tin. in 2020, the country's share of the world's mined cobalt production amounted to 69%; tantalum, 37%; diamond, 12%; mined
copper, 8%; tin, 7%; refined copper, 5%; and gold, 1%. In 2020, Congo (Kinshasa) accounted for about 51% of the world's cobalt reserves. Crude petroleum production also played a significant role in the domestic economy. The country was not
a globally significant consumer of minerals or mineral fuels in 2020 (Republique Democratique du Congo Ministere des Mines, 2021, p. 28; Flanagan, 2022; Friedline, 2022; Merrill, 2022;
Olson, 2022a, b; Sheaffer, 2022; Shedd, 2021, 2022).
Minerals in the National Economy
  The mining and mineral-processing sector accounted for an estimated 13.6% of the gross domestic product (gDP) in 2019 (the latest year for which data were available), and the manufacturing sector, 18.8%. The value of real output in the
mining and mineral-processing sector increased by 1% in 2019 compared with an increase of 16.9% in 2018. In 2019, the mining and mineral-processing sector accounted for 6.4% of the growth in the GDP compared with 75.6% in 2018 and 52.5% in 2017 (Banque Centrale du Congo, 2020, p. 32-33, 35).
  About 450,000 artisanal miners were estimated to be employed in diamond mining in Congo (Kinshasa) in 2016. Between 150,000 and 200,000 artisanal miners were estimated to be employed in cobalt and copper mining in 2019. gold mining was estimated to employ more than 270,000 artisanal miners in 2019; tin mining, 95,000; niobium and tantalum mining, a total of 26,000; tungsten mining, 7,700; and tourmaline mining, 2,000. in the fourth quarter of 2020, niobium, tantalum, tin, and tungsten mining employed a total of about 38,100 miners in the mine sites covered by the iTRi Tin supply Chain initiative (iTsCi) (James, 2016; international Tin association, 2021b; Delve, undated, p. 17).
Government Policies and Programs
in March 2018, the President of Congo (Kinshasa) signed
law No. 18/001 of 9 March 2018, which was the county's new mining code. in February 2019, the government issued a decree that banned the export of cobalt and copper concentrates to promote domestic downstream processing of cobalt and copper. In March, the Government lifted the ban; the Government's decision could be attributable to power shortages limiting downstream processing of concentrates. The Government also announced its intentions to review its decision regarding the ban every 6 months. in august 2020, the government announced waivers from the export bans on cassiterite, cobalt carbonate,

cobalt hydroxide, columbite-tantalite, copper concentrates, and
wolframite (Radford and others, 2019; Holland, 2020). Congo (Kinshasa) was a signatory to the Kimberley Process
Certification Scheme, which is a certification system that became effective on January 1, 2003, to reduce the trade in conflict diamond. Congo (Kinshasa) also was a signatory to the
Extractive Industries Transparency Initiative, which promotes the open and accountable management of minerals and mineral fuels. in July 2010, the United states Congress passed the Dodd-
Frank Wall street Reform and Consumer Protection act (Dodd-Frank Act) which contains provisions concerning the use of minerals to finance military operations in eastern Congo (Kinshasa). The U.s. securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) issued regulations in final form in accordance with the Dodd-Frank Wall street Reform and Consumer Protection act in august 2012.
  in March 2011, the government of Katanga Province and the international Tin association (formerly the international Tin Research Institute) started the iTSCI, which is a traceability mechanism for domestically produced tantalum, tin, and tungsten to meet end users' requirements under the Dodd- Frank act and organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development due diligence guidelines. Of the 355 mine sites covered by the iTSCI in the former Katanga Province (which was divided into Haut-Katanga, Haut-Lomami, Lualaba,
and Tanganyika Provinces in 2015). In the fourth quarter of 2020, 171 mines were active. In Maniema Province, 205
active and 270 inactive mine sites were covered by the iTSCI in the fourth quarter of 2020. in nord-Kivu Province, 121 active and 83 inactive mine sites were covered. In Sud-Kivu Province, 135 active and 59 inactive mine sites were covered (international Tin association, 2021b).
  armed groups, including Congo (Kinshasa) military units and various local groups, reportedly interfered with artisanal mining operations in eastern Congo (Kinshasa) through illegal taxation of miners. in 2019, these armed groups reportedly interfered with at least 33% of the artisanal mining operations in Ituri, nord-Kivu, and sud-Kivu Provinces. interference by armed groups was rare in the former Katanga Province (Matthysen and others, 2019a, p. 7, 18-19).
Production
  In 2020, the production of zinc increased by 852%; germanium, by an estimated 367%; smelted copper, by an estimated 92%; tin, by an estimated 46%; niobium and tantalum, by an estimated 37% each; tourmaline, by an estimated 29%; mined copper, by an estimated 22%; and refined copper, by 18%. Between 2016 and 2020, cement production increased
by 407%; smelted copper, by an estimated 358%; tin, by an estimated 131%; and refined copper, by 67%. Tungsten

production decreased by an estimated 54% in 2020. Lead and manganese mining shut down in 2019 (Republique Democratique du Congo Ministere des Mines, 2020; 2021,
p. 62, 66; Banque Centrale du Congo, 2022, p. 1).
  Smelted copper production increased in 2020 because new smelters opened. The increase in tin production was attributable to the recent startup of large-scale mining operations that more than offset decreased artisanal production. Data on mineral production are in table 1.
Structure of the Mineral Industry
  in surveys conducted by the international Peace information Service (IPIS) between 2013 and 2019, gold was produced in ituri, the former Katanga, Maniema, nord-Kivu, and sud-Kivu Provinces at a minimum of 1,759 mines; niobium, tantalum, and tungsten, at a minimum of 911 mines; diamond, at a minimum of 43 mines; and tourmaline, at a minimum of 8 mines. some mines produced at least two different minerals (Matthysen
and others, 2019b).
  La Générale des Carričres et des Mines (Gécamines), which was a state-owned company, produced coal, cobalt, copper, and sulfuric acid. Other cobalt and copper mining companies were privately owned; Gécamines held shares of between 5% and 40% in numerous operations. Large-scale gold mines were also privately owned. The Government held shares in the country's large-scale diamond producers, including 80% in société Miničre de Bakwanga (MIBA) and a 50% share in Societe Anhui-Congo d'Investissment Minier SPRL (SACIM).
  artisanal and small-scale miners accounted for most or all of Congo (Kinshasa)'s output of diamond, gemstones, niobium, tantalum, and tungsten. artisanal and small-scale miners also played a significant role in the country's cobalt, gold, and tin mine production (Matthysen and others, 2019b; Republique Democratique du Congo Ministere des Mines, 2021, p. 5, 22, 41, 43, 46, 62; Sawasawa, 2021). Capacity, location, ownership, and production information were not readily available for many artisanal and small-scale operations. Table 2 is a list of major mineral industry facilities.
Mineral Trade
  Total reported exports were valued at $15 billion in 2019, and imports, at $14.6 billion. Copper accounted for 56.6% of the value of Congo (Kinshasa)'s exports; cobalt, 28%; gold, 7.6%; crude petroleum, 3.8%; diamond, 1.1%; and lead, niobium, tantalum, tin, and zinc, a total of 2.1%. other mineral exports included germanium, tourmaline, and tungsten (Banque Centrale du Congo, 2020, p. 104, 106).
  In 2020, the share of Congo (Kinshasa)'s copper production that was refined prior to export was 79%. An additional 7% was smelted prior to export (table 1). Cobalt mine production was processed into intermediate products, such as cobalt carbonate and cobalt hydroxide, and then exported. Most or all Congo (Kinshasa)'s diamond, niobium, tantalum, tin, and tungsten production was exported prior to downstream processing.

Industrial Minerals
  Cement and Lime.-national cement production decreased to about 1.28 million metric tons (Mt) in 2020 from a revised

1.38 Mt in 2019. Lower production was attributable to the effects of the COVID-19 pandemic. In September, Zijin and Lualaba Carrilu opened a new cement plant near Kolwezi that had a capacity of 800,000 t/yr (Global Cement, 2020b; Banque Centrale du Congo, 2022, p. 4).
  in late september 2020, the government of Tanganyika announced the start of construction of a new cement plant in Kabimba with a capacity of 2 million metric tons per year (Mt/yr). In May, Sinoma International Engineering Co. Ltd. of China announced the start of construction of a new cement plant in Lubudi. The plant's planned capacity was 1 Mt/yr
of cement and 200,000 t/yr of lime. It was unclear when the plants in Kabimba and Lubudi would be completed (Global Cement, 2020a; Seetao News, 2020).
  Diamond.-artisanal and small-scale miners accounted for most of Congo (Kinshasa)'s output of diamond. In 2020, artisanal and small-scale diamond production was
8.5 million carats compared with 9.5 million carats in 2019 and 11.9 million carats in 2018. Production in Kasai-oriental Province decreased to 7.11 million carats in 2020 from
8.01 million carats in 2019; in Kasai Central Province, to about 456,000 carats from 521,000 carats; and in Sankuru Province, to less than 6,000 carats from 333,000 carats. in Kasai Province, production increased to about 856,000 carats in 2020 from 512,000 carats in 2019 (Republique Democratique du Congo Ministere des Mines, 2019; 2020; 2021, p. 4).
  saCiM (anhui Foreign Economic Construction group of China, 50%, and Government-owned Société Congolaise d'Investissment Minier, 50%) produced mostly industrial- quality diamond at the Tshibwe Mine in Kasai-Oriental
Province. in 2020, saCiM produced about 4.39 million carats compared with 3.98 million carats in 2019 and 3.08 million carats in 2018. The company was the leading diamond- producing company in Congo (Kinshasa) (Republique Democratique du Congo Ministere des Mines, 2019;
2020; 2021, p. 2, 9).
  MiBa mined diamond at Mbuji-Mayi in Kasai-oriental Province. in 2020, the company produced 37,447 carats from its alluvial deposits compared with 19,683 carats in 2019 and 118,788 carats in 2018. MIBA's operations were on care-and- maintenance status between March and November 2019 and
between January and September 2020 (Republique Democratique
du Congo Ministere des Mines, 2019, 2020; 2021, p. 2).
  Lithium.-aVZ Minerals Ltd. of australia completed a scoping study on a new mine at its Manono project in May 2019 and a feasibility study in april 2020. Planned production in the feasibility study was 700,000 t/yr of spodumene concentrates at a grade of 6% lithium oxide and 46,000 t/yr of primary lithium sulfate. The company also was considering the production
of tantalum and tin. Depending on the results of AVZ's final investment decision, construction could start in the third quarter of 2021. Shipments of concentrates could start in the first quarter of 2023, and those of lithium sulfate, in the third quarter of 2023. The estimated life of Manono, which is in Tanganyika Province, was estimated to be at least 20 years (AVZ Minerals Ltd., 2021b, p. 6-7).

MINERAL INDUSTRY HIGHLIGHTS IN 2021

In 2021, the nominal GDP of Congo (Kinshasa) was about
$52.3 billion. The mining and mineral-processing sector accounted for 13.8% of the gDP in 2020; the real value of output in the mining and mineral-processing sector increased by 9.7% in 2020. Total exports were nearly $13.8 billion in 2020, of which copper accounted for 68%; cobalt, 16%; gold, 9%; crude petroleum, 3%; diamond, 1%; and other minerals, 2%
(Banque Centrale du Congo, 2021, p. 30, 96, 99; 2022, p. 1).
Production
  in 2021, the production of cement, mined cobalt, and germanium increased by 21% each (table 1). Manganese production restarted in 2021. increased cement production in 2021 could be attributable to the opening of Zijin and Lualaba Carrilu's new plant in Kolwezi. Mined cobalt production increased because of the opening of the Deziwa and the Pumpi Mines and the reopening of the Mutanda Mine. germanium production increased as Big Hill ramped up towards
its full capacity.
Commodity Review

Metals
  Cobalt and Copper.-in 2021, mined copper production increased to 1.8 Mt from 1.71 Mt in 2020, and mined cobalt production, to 119,000 t from 98,000 t. Katanga Mining produced 264,400 t of refined copper and 23,800 t of cobalt in hydroxide
in 2021 compared with 270,700 t of refined copper and 23,900 t of cobalt in hydroxide in 2020. Production was limited by power outages (glencore plc, 2022, p. 7, 11).
  glencore restarted cobalt and copper production at the Mutanda Mine in the second and fourth quarters of 2021, respectively. By yearend, the company produced 6,300 t of refined copper and 3,900 t of cobalt in hydroxide (glencore plc, 2022, p. 11).
  In 2021, production at Tenke Fungurume was 209,120 t of refined copper and 18,501 t of cobalt in hydroxide compared with 182,597 t of refined copper and 15,436 t of cobalt in hydroxide in 2020. China Molybdenum and gécamines started trial production from an expansion of Tenke Fungurume's capacity by nearly 89,000 t/yr of copper and 7,300 t/yr of cobalt in July. The companies planned to expand capacity by an additional 200,000 t/yr of copper and 17,000 t/yr of cobalt by 2023 (Mining Technology, 2021; Ross, 2022).
  in June 2021, sicomines completed the expansion of its capacity at Mashamba West to nearly 254,000 t/yr of refined copper and 5,180 t/yr of cobalt in hydroxide. Sicomines produced 194,802 t of copper and 1,316 t of cobalt in
2021 (Agence Congolaise de Presse, 2021; China Railway
group Ltd., 2022, p. 31).
  In 2021, ERG's production of refined copper at RTR increased to 91,048 t from 80,300 t in 2020 and cobalt in hydroxide, to 17,400 t from 17,000 t. ERg also produced 103,300 t of copper in concentrate at Frontier in 2020 (Eurasian Resources group,
2021, p. 12, 26; Darton Commodities Ltd., 2022, p. 9; Republique Democratique du Congo Ministere des Mines, 2022, p. 47).
  
COMMUS produced 121,072 t of copper at Kolwezi in 2021, of which 59,928 t was refined. In 2020, the company produced 114,265 t of copper, of which 57,400 t was refined. Cobalt production increased to 1,607 t in 2021 from 1,423 t in 2020.
Planned production for 2022 was 127,000 t of copper and nearly 2,000 t of cobalt (Zijin Mining group Co. Ltd., 2021, p. 34, 40-41; 2022, p. 37, 44).
  CNMC produced 84,173 t of refined copper at its operations in Haut-Katanga Province in 2021 compared with 80,215 t in 2020. CNMC Huachin Mining Mabende's production of refined copper decreased to 36,389 t in 2021 from 36,632 t in 2020. Production by Huachin Metal Leach SA increased to 29,025 t in 2021 from 26,738 t in 2020, and that by CNMC Congo, to 18,759 t from 16,845 t. In December 2021, CNMC was producing smelted copper at the LCS at the rate of nearly 65,000 t/yr compared with 62,000 t/yr in December 2020 (Division Provinciale des Mines de haut-Katanga, 2021; Division Provinciale des Mines de Lualaba, 2021, 2022; Republique Democratique du Congo Ministere des Mines, 2022, p. 47).
  CNMC and Gécamines produced 62,397 t of refined copper at Deziwa in 2021. Cobalt production at Deziwa increased to about 7,800 t in 2021 from 4,200 t in 2020. gécamines also produced about 4,400 t of cobalt at Big Hill in 2021 compared with 3,600 t in 2020 (Darton Commodities Ltd., 2022, p. 9; Republique Democratique du Congo Ministere des Mines, 2022, p. 47).
  In late March 2021, Gécamines officially started its new subsidiary Entreprise Générale du Cobalt (EGC), which would have a monopoly on the purchasing, processing, and sale of cobalt produced by artisanal miners in Congo (Kinshasa). as of the
end of september 2021, companies could purchase artisanally- produced cobalt from EgC. Underground mining operations would not be allowed on EGC's mining sites and open pit mines were allowed a maximum depth of 10 meters (Le Bec, 2021).
  EgC planned to purchase 7,000 t of cobalt in hydroxide from artisanal miners at Kasulo in 2021, 15,000 t in 2022, 20,000 t in 2023, and 25,000 t in 2025. In late September, EGC and CDM were in a dispute regarding EGC's plans for CDM to relinquish its mining rights at Kasulo. as of yearend, EgC had not purchased any cobalt (Le Bec, 2021; Reid, 2021, 2022).
  CDM's production of refined copper decreased to 56,131 t in 2021 from 66,803 t in 2020. The company's production of
cobalt in hydroxide at Kasulo decreased to about 3,200 t in 2021 from 4,400 t in 2020 (Division Provinciale des Mines de haut- Katanga, 2021; Darton Commodities Ltd., 2022, p. 9; Republique Democratique du Congo Ministere des Mines, 2022, p. 47).
  ivanhoe started mining at Kakula and Kamoa in late May 2021 and produced 105,884 t of copper in concentrate by yearend. The company planned to produce between 290,000 and 340,000 t in 2022, with the second phase of mining likely to start in the second quarter of 2022. Total planned production in the first and second phases of mining was more than 400,000 t/yr. Depending on a prefeasibility study that was planned to be completed in the third quarter of 2022, ivanhoe could start the third phase of mining by the end of 2024. Ivanhoe also planned to build a new smelter in the third phase with a capacity of 500,000 t/yr that would process the majority of concentrates produced at Kakula and Kamoa (ivanhoe Mines Ltd., 2022, p. 1-2, 8-9, 18).

in 2021, Jinchuan produced 33,063 t of copper and 3,379 t
of cobalt at Ruashi compared with 33,897 t of refined copper and 4,158 t of contained cobalt in 2020. The company produced 28,197 t of copper in concentrate at Kinsenda compared
with 30,557 t in 2020. Decreased production at Jinchuan and Kinsenda was attributable to lower ore grades (Jinchuan Group International Resources Co. Ltd., 2022a, p. 14-15, 17-18).
  MMG produced 48,017 t of refined copper at Kinsevere in 2021 compared with 72,007 t in 2020. Mining shut down in December 2020; the company processed ore stockpiles in 2021.
MMg planned to restart mining of the remaining oxide ores
in April 2022 and to produce between 45,000 and 50,000 t of refined copper in 2022. As of yearend, MMG was engaged in a feasibility study on mining copper and cobalt from sulfide ores at Kinsevere (MMg Ltd., 2022, p. 6).
  In 2021, Wanbao and Managem produced 38,508 t of refined copper at Pumpi compared with 9,646 t in 2020. The companies also started producing cobalt in 2021 and produced 3,186 t of cobalt in hydroxide by yearend. Comika produced an estimated 4,200 t of cobalt at Kamoya in 2021 compared with 2,900 t in 2020. The company also produced 33,379 t of refined copper in 2020 compared with 4,055 t in 2019 (Division Provinciale des Mines de haut-Katanga, 2021; Darton Commodities Ltd., 2022,
p. 9; Managem s.a., 2022, p. 116; Republique Democratique du Congo Ministere des Mines, 2022, p. 47).
  Chemaf produced 19,541 t of refined copper in 2021 compared with 23,770 t in 2020. The company's cobalt production decreased to 5,300 t in 2021 from about 6,200 t in 2020 (Division Provinciale des Mines de haut-Katanga, 2021; Darton Commodities Ltd., 2022, p. 9; Republique Democratique du Congo Ministere des Mines, 2022, p. 47).
  Gold.-AngloGold Ashanti's and Barrick's production at the Kibali Mine was nearly unchanged at 25,300 kg in 2021.
The companies planned to produce between 23,500 and 26,300 kg in 2022 and to continue producing nearly 25,000 kg/yr through
at least 2026. The estimated remaining life of Kibali was at least
10 years (Barrick gold Corp., 2022, p. 31-32, 63, 86).
  Niobium (Columbium) and Tantalum.-in 2021, national columbite-tantalite output was 1,441 t compared with 1,712 t in 2020 and 1,313 t in 2019. Production by artisanal miners in nord-Kivu Province decreased to 1,106 t in 2021 from 1,171 t in 2020 and in sud-Kivu Province, to 182 t from 222 t. societe Miniere de Bisunzu also produced 149 t of columbite-tantalite in Nord-Kivu Province in 2021; the company was Congo (Kinshasa)'s only large-scale producer. The estimated tantalum content of cassiterite was 430 t in 2021 (World Mining, 1979; Ellis, 1981; Republique Democratique du Congo Ministere des Mines, 2021, p. 43; 2022, p. 29-30, 42).
  Tin.-Cassiterite production increased to 28,628 t in 2021 from 26,678 t in 2020. alphamin produced 19,021 t of cassiterite in 2021 compared with 17,559 t in 2020. The tin content of Alphamin's cassiterite was 10,969 t in 2021; the
company planned to increase production to 12,000 t in 2022. Cassiterite production by artisanal and small-scale miners remained nearly unchanged at 9,094 t in 2021 (Republique Democratique du Congo Ministere des Mines, 2021, p. 39, 41; 2022, p. 27-28; international Tin association, 2022).

Industrial Minerals
  Diamond.-saCiM produced 3.61 million carats in 2021 compared with 4.39 million carats in 2020. MIBA's production increased to 148,650 carats in 2021 from 37,447 carats in 2020 as the company remained in operation through the entire year (Republique Democratique du Congo Ministere des Mines, 2021, p. 2; 2022, p. 2).
  in 2021, artisanal and small-scale diamond production increased to 9.21 million carats from 8.52 million carats in 2020. Production in Kasai-oriental Province increased
to 8.72 million carats in 2021 from 7.11 million carats in 2020. in Kasai Province, production decreased to about 224,000 carats in 2021 from 856,000 carats in 2020 (Republique Democratique
du Congo Ministere des Mines, 2021, p. 4; 2022, p. 4).
  Lithium.-Depending on the results of AVZ's final investment decision for the Manono project that was rescheduled for the fourth quarter of 2021, construction could start in the first quarter of 2022. Shipments of lithium concentrates could start in the second quarter of 2023 and
lithium sulfate, in the fourth quarter of 2023. as of yearend, the final investment decision had not been made (AVZ Minerals Ltd., 2021a, p. 6).
Outlook
  Cobalt and copper production are expected to increase because of the startup of the Deziwa and the Pumpi Mines in 2020, the Kakula and the Kamoa Mines in 2021, and the Musonoi Mine in 2022 and because of the completion of the
expansions of the Mashamba West and the Tenke Fungurume Mines in 2021. Tin production is expected to increase in 2022 because of the Bisie project. Cement production is likely to increase in 2022 because of the new plant near Kolwezi; further increases could result depending on the opening of plants in Lualaba and Tanganyika Provinces. Lithium production could start in 2023 with the opening of the Manono project. Diamond mining also could increase as Tshibwe ramps up to full capacity.
  The development of these projects depends heavily upon political and economic stability and favorable conditions in world markets. The outlook for gold, niobium, tantalum, tin, and tungsten is particularly dependent upon political stability because of continued civil unrest in eastern Congo (Kinshasa) and upon international concerns about the reported use of minerals to finance military operations.
